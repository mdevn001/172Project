Advisory Boards

INPUT FROM ENGINEERING LEADERS
OFFERING STUDENTS A REWARDING EXPERIENCE

The Dean's Council of Advisors comprises senior leaders from business, industry, government, and academia, active or retired, who represent the broad spectrum of regional, statewide, and national high-technology industries. The council provides the dean of the Marlan & Rosemary Bourns College of Engineering with an external perspective on the college's programs and activities; identifies issues important to the success of these initiatives; provides guidance, counsel, consultation, and recommendations to the dean; reviews curricula for relevance and research for purpose and progress; and provides other assistance and support as required by the dean.

Gordon Bourns

Chairman and CEO, Bourns, Inc.
Chairman, BCOE Council of Advisors

Mr. Bourns is the chairman of the board and CEO of Bourns, Inc. Headquartered in Riverside, Bourns, Inc. is a leading manufacturer and supplier of sensors, magnets, and other microelectronic products. Gordon’s parents, Marlan and Rosemary, named the UCR engineering school in 1994.  Mr. Bourns has served on the UCR Foundation Board of Trustees since 1984, and was the chair from 1997-1999. 

Mark H. Boyd

Technical Fellow, Software, The Boeing Co.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mark Boyd  is a Boeing Technical Fellow and the Chief Software Engineer for a large Boeing AvionX software project, whose key areas of technical expertise include Software Architecture and Engineering (embedded and enterprise), User Experience/User Interfaces and Visualization, and Machine Learning. Mark holds both Bachelor of Science and Master of Science degrees in computer science from UCR.

John Brisco

Vice President. Irvine Pipeline Company, Inc.

John studied at Riverside City College and Cal State Fullerton. He joined the Irvine Pipeline Company part-time in 1978 and has worked there full-time since 1984. They build public and private infrastructure focused on water, sewer, and storm drain systems. John has a strong interested in STEM education and has served as a mentor for the Pick group. He was a board member of Riverside Educational Enrichment Fund (REEF). He's also a past president and served many other volunteer roles at the Victoria Club.

Wallace Brithinee

Consultant and former President, Brithinee Electric
Board Member, Manufacturers' Council IE
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Wallace Brithinee served as president of Brithinee Electric until Sulzer acquired the company in 2018. He is a consultant and chair of the Manufacturers’ Council of the Inland Empire. Wally and his twin brother Don, both attended UCR and hold PhD’s in Mathematics.  Wally served as a member of the Department of Mechanical Engineering Advisory Board before joining the Dean’s Advisory Council.

Ruth Chao

General Affairs Manager, Delta Electronics (Americas) Ltd.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Ms. Chao is an alumna of UCR and is currently the General Affairs Manager at Delta Electronics (Americas) Ltd.  The company manufactures energy systems, vehicle starter systems, and batteries.  Ms. Chao oversaw construction of a 178,000 sq.ft. headquarters in the city of Fremont.  She lives in the San Francisco Bay Area.

Winston Chung

Chairman, Winston Global Energy
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors 

Mr. Chung is the founder and chairman of Winston Global Energy Ltd. which manufactures and markets energy storage solutions and lithium batteries.  He also serves as director and chief scientist of China’s Lithium Batter Research and Development Center.  In 2011, Mr. Chung gave UCR’s largest gift to-date from an individual to name Winston Chung Hall and establish the Winston Chung Global Energy Center at UCR. 

Michael Clinch

Senior Consultant and Materials Development Group Leader, Innoval Technology
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Clinch is currently a senior consultant and materials development group leader at Innoval Technology in Banbury, Oxfordshire, UK.  The company provides high-quality consultancy and technical support to investors, manufacturers and end-users of aluminum and other metals.  Mr. Clinch has served as chair of the Light Metals Division at the IOM3 organization since 2014.  He is also a former member of the BCOE MS&E Board of Advisors. 

William "Bill" Dull 

President, Triad Magnetics
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Bill Dull is a leader in the power magnetics field with more than 35 years of experience in the industry. His background leading North American divisions of Japanese, Chinese and Taiwanese companies has provided expertise in the global market, with key business and marketing strengths in the magnetics arena.

In his role as president of Triad Magnetics for the past 15 years, Dull manages a diversified portfolio of standard power transformers, inductors and power supplies, as well as Triad’s U.S. Design Center in Perris, Calif., which provides extensive custom design services.

Nora Hackett

Visiting Scholar, Department of Botany and Plant Science - University of California, Riverside
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Nora Hackett is currently a visiting scholar for the Department of Botany and Plant Sciences at UCR.  Prior to this role, she was the Director of intellectual property services at UCR, and the campus liaison officer at UC Davis’ Office of Research.  Dr. Hackett established the Colin E. Hackett Award in honor of her late husband to provide research support to full-time graduate students in engineering and the Nora A. Hackett Instructional Equipment Fund. She also serves as a member of the UCR Foundation Board of Trustees.  

 

Michael Hartnett

Chairman, CEO and President, RBC Bearings, Inc.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Dr. Hartnett currently serves as the chairman, CEO and president of RBC Bearings, Inc., an international manufacturer and marketer of highly engineered precision plain, roller and ball bearings.  Dr. Hartnett has developed numerous patents and has authored dozens of papers.  He is well-respected in the field of tribology, the study of friction.  Dr. Hartnett has served in many other roles at various engineering firms, such as Aftermarket Tech. Corp. and Process Fab, Inc. Dr. Hatnett lives in Oxford, Connecticut. 
 

Brian Hawley

Chairman and CTO, Luminex Software, Inc.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Hawley is currently chairman and CTO of Luminex Software, Inc. in Riverside. The company is a provider of mainframe data and connectivity solutions and is known for its industry-leading Mainframe Virtual Tape solutions.  Mr. Hawley is an alumnus of UCR and current chair of the UCR Foundation Board of Trustees.  He is also a member of several other advisory boards within BCOE and at the School of Business.



 

Steven Henderson

Vice President of Business Development, Invetech

Steven Henderson currently serves as VP of Business Development with Invetech (a Fortive company) for the Western United States and Asia Pacific Regions.  In this role, Steven is responsible for driving business partnerships and growth of the Design & Engineering and Contract Manufacturing Professional Service portfolio. 
Steven is the first Black to earn an undergraduate degree in chemical engineering from The Bourns College of Engineering at University of California Riverside in 1994, and received an MBA from the Peter F. Drucker School of Business Management in 2007. He is a licensed and registered professional engineer in the State of California. He has over 20 years of experience in biotechnology. Prior to joining Invetech, he served at Thermo Fisher Scientific (formerly Life Technologies/Invitrogen) for the 10 years in various leadership roles throughout the organization. 
As a Principal Consulting Engineer, he contributed his talents to numerous  biotechnology, pharmaceutical drug development efforts and in-vitro diagnostic (IVD) device manufacturing companies. His specializations are Drug Development, Cell Therapy, Program Management, Quality Systems, Facility Remediation, Validation, Business Development and Process Improvement. Steven is a founding board member of the Council for the Advancement of Black Engineers (CABE), serving as the founding President and Chairman since its inception in 2012, and currently in the role of Chief of Staff on the CABE Advisory Board. 
 

William "Bill" Johnson

President and CEO, Johnson Machinery Co.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Johnson served as president and CEO of Johnson Machinery from 1969 to 2017 when the company was acquired by Quinn Catt.  The company was founded by his father and headquartered in Riverside since 1940.  The company is a product provider for Caterpillar and Hyster Lift.  Mr. Johnson has served on multiple advisory boards at UCR, and, along with his wife Sue Johnson who serves on the Foundation Board, has established several endowed funds across campus. 

David Key

SaaS Thought Leader, Cloud Strategies, Agile Equity, Corent Technology
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Key is the managing director of Cloud Strategies which provides strategic advisory and SaaS execution assistance services to SaaS companies.  Mr. Key previously was on the founding team of FileNet (acquired by IBM for $1.6B), FieldCentrix, and YouMail.  Mr. Key has extensive experience in the cloud-based software industry.  He is an alumnus of UCR and has cultivated close relationships with UCR since graduating.  He currently lives in Laguna Hills, CA.

Robert "Bob" Krieger

Chairman of the Board and Principal Engineer, Krieger and Stewart, Inc.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Krieger is the chairman of the board, founder, and principal engineer of Krieger & Stewart, Inc., an environmental engineering firm in Riverside that provides services in water and wastewater related issues.  Mr. Krieger attended UCR in the 50’s and completed his degree at Berkeley.  Mr. Krieger has remained active with UCR for many decades, serving on multiple advisory boards and is an emeritus member of the UCR Foundation Board of Trustees.

Ernest Levister

Physician, Private Practice
Vice Chairman, BCOE Council of Advisors

Dr. Levister is a physician specializing in internal medicine and occupational medicine in San Bernardino.  He is also a member of the School of Medicine – Community Advisory Board.  Dr. Levister helped establish AfricanAmericans United in Science organization at UCR and was the commencement keynote speaker in 2014.  Dr. Levister was previously an instructor at several universities in the US, and served in the Army Medical Corps and as an attache in the State Department. 

Ching Liu

Executive Vice President, SolarMax Technology Inc and Inland Empire Renewable Energy Regional Center, LLC.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Ms. Liu currently serves as the executive vice president of SolarMax Technology, Inc. and Inland Empire Renewable Energy Regional Center, LLC.  SolarMax is a full-service photovoltaic solar energy systems integrator for commercial and residential customers.  Ms. Liu is a member of several advisory boards at UCR, including BCOE and School of Business and helped establish the Solarmax Endowed Graduate Fellowship at the School of Business and has supported a variety of BCOE programs. 

Kevin Marcus

Founder and CTO of Versium Analytics, Inc.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Marcus is currently the founder and CTO of Versium Analytics, Inc., a data technology company in Redmond, WA that operates a LifeData® predictive analytics scoring solution.  Mr. Marcus also owns and operations several internet properties and several investment vehicles and equity trading systems.  Mr. Marcus attended UCR in the 90’s and has supported a variety of programs at BCOE. He was also a guest lecturer. 

Eric Sheppard

Program Director, National Science Foundation
Research Associate Professor, Department of Engineering, Hampton University
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Dr. Sheppard is currently a research associate professor in the Department of Engineering at Hampton University in Virginia.  His focus is on engineering education, aerospace engineering, space propulsion and aircraft design.  He was previously the Dean of the School of Engineering and Technology at Hampton University from 2004-2016 and was an associate professor at Tuskegee University.

Victor Tolan

President and CEO, B&K Precision Corporation
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Tolan is currently the president and CEO of B&K Precision Corporation, which is headquartered in Yorba Linda and provides test and measurement instruments for engineering, electronic design and education.  Mr. Tolan assumed leadership after acquiring the company with Japanese and French investors.  He helped expand the company into Europe and Asia.  Mr. Tolan is a School of Business Executive Fellow and has spoken at their Dean’s Speaker Series. 

Shane Trussell

President, Trussell Technologies, Inc.
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Dr. Trussell is currently the president of Trussell Technologies, Inc., an environmental engineering firm focused on process and water quality.  Dr. Trussell is a registered civil engineer in California and has focused on water, seawater, and wastewater treatment throughout his career.  He has more than 57 publications and presentations and is a UCR alumnus.  He lives in San Diego, CA. 

David Tsai

Partner, Pillsbury Winthrop Shaw Pittman LLP
Member, Bourns College of Engineering Council of Advisors

Mr. Tsai is a partner in Pillsbury Winthrop Shaw Pittman LLP's San Francisco office and co-head of the firm's Taiwan practice. His practice focuses on intellectual property, patent, trade secret, trademark, copyright, complex breach of contract, and product defect litigation in courts and international arbitration tribunals. His late father was a professor of Mechanical Engineering at BCOE, and the Tsai family established the Lung-Wen Tsai Mechanical Design Fund for graduate students, in his memory in 2004.  Mr. Tsai has served on the UCR Foundation Board of Trustees since July 2015.
